This is One Touch Notes, a reimplementation of the great Tombo notes taker we once had on Windows Mobile. It allows you to organize any number of text notes in a hierarchy of folders and subfolders, yet access any note from the list with a single tap.

One Touch Notes respects your privacy by storing everything exclusively on your device. It does not require network access and will never collect or transfer data outside your device.

If you want to thank the developer, or if you need more features, you might want to have a look at Memo Hub.

One Touch Notes is open source, and you find its repository here. The notes list is based on Bogdan Melnychuk's great AndroidTreeView library and displays icons using Evgeny Shishkin's elegant Print library and a Fontello generated icon font with glyphs from the beautiful Font Awesome by Dave Gandy.
